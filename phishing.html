What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack that attempts to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted organization. Cybercriminals usually carry out phishing attacks through fraudulent emails, fake websites, or deceptive messages that appear legitimate at first glance. According to Anderson and Rainie (2020), phishing is one of the most common cyber threats because it relies on human trust rather than technical system vulnerabilities. Attackers often disguise themselves as banks, online services, or educational platforms in order to convince users that the message is authentic. When users click on the provided links or submit personal information, the data is immediately captured by the attacker.

Students are particularly vulnerable to phishing attacks because they frequently receive emails related to school activities, online learning platforms, and social media notifications. For example, a phishing email might claim that a student's school account has been locked and request them to log in through a provided link. The link leads to a fake login page designed to look identical to the real website, causing students to unknowingly give away their account credentials. Once attackers gain access to the account, they may steal personal information, send spam messages, or attempt further cyberattacks using the compromised account. Recognizing suspicious messages and verifying the authenticity of links is therefore essential for preventing phishing attacks.
