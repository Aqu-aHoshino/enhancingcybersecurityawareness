What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a cyberattack technique that manipulates individuals into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise their digital security. Instead of exploiting technical system vulnerabilities, attackers rely on psychological manipulation to deceive users into trusting them. According to cybersecurity research, social engineering attacks often involve impersonating trusted individuals such as teachers, company representatives, or technical support personnel. Attackers may request passwords, financial information, or access to sensitive systems under the false claim that the request is necessary for legitimate purposes. Because these attacks rely on human behavior rather than software vulnerabilities, they can be difficult to detect.

Students may encounter social engineering attempts through messages claiming to be from school administrators or online service providers. For example, an attacker might send a message asking the student to verify their account information or download a file that supposedly contains important school documents. Once the student follows the instructions, the attacker may gain access to the student’s personal data or install malicious software on their device. Recognizing suspicious requests and verifying the identity of individuals before sharing sensitive information are important strategies for preventing social engineering attacks.
