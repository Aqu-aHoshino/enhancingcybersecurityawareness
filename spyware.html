What is Spyware?

Spyware is a type of malicious software designed to secretly monitor and collect information about a user's activities without their knowledge or consent. This software may record browsing behavior, capture keystrokes, or gather personal information such as login credentials and financial data. According to cybersecurity experts, spyware often operates silently in the background of a computer system, making it difficult for users to detect its presence. Once installed, spyware can transmit the collected data to cybercriminals who may use the information for identity theft or financial fraud. Because of its hidden nature, spyware represents a serious threat to personal privacy and digital security.

Students may unknowingly install spyware when downloading free applications, games, or browser extensions from untrusted websites. For example, a seemingly harmless program might secretly include spyware that records the user's online activities and sends the data to attackers. In some cases, spyware may also slow down device performance or cause unexpected system behavior. Installing trusted security software and avoiding downloads from suspicious sources can significantly reduce the risk of spyware infections. Awareness of spyware threats is essential for protecting personal data in digital environments.
