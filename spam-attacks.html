What is Spam?

Spam refers to unwanted or unsolicited digital messages that are sent to large numbers of users through email, messaging platforms, or social media. These messages often contain advertisements, promotional offers, or malicious links designed to trick users into visiting harmful websites. According to cybersecurity research, spam messages are frequently used as a delivery method for phishing attacks and malware infections. Because spam messages are distributed in large volumes, they increase the chances that some recipients will interact with the content. This makes spam an effective tool for cybercriminals attempting to spread digital threats.

Students may receive spam emails promising free rewards, scholarships, or online promotions that appear attractive but are actually fraudulent. Clicking on the links within these messages may lead to phishing websites or initiate downloads of malicious software. In some cases, spam messages may also request personal information such as passwords or account details. Ignoring suspicious messages and avoiding unknown links are important steps in preventing spam-related cyber threats. Email filtering systems and spam detection tools can also help reduce the number of unwanted messages reaching users.
