What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the protection of digital systems, networks, and information from cyber threats such as hacking, malware, and unauthorized access. As technology becomes increasingly integrated into education, schools rely heavily on digital platforms for communication, research, and online learning activities. According to Giannakas et al. (2019), cybersecurity involves the use of tools, policies, and technologies designed to maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information systems. These protective measures are necessary to ensure that sensitive data, including personal information and academic records, remains secure from unauthorized users or cybercriminals. Without proper cybersecurity practices, individuals and organizations may become vulnerable to attacks that compromise digital privacy and safety.

Cybersecurity awareness is an important component of digital safety because it helps individuals understand cyber risks and learn how to respond effectively to online threats. Cybersecurity awareness includes recognizing suspicious online activities, identifying phishing attempts, and applying preventive strategies such as using strong passwords and protecting personal data. Research indicates that individuals who have higher levels of cybersecurity awareness are more likely to behave responsibly in digital environments and are less likely to become victims of cybercrime (Kovacevic, Putnik, & Toskovic, 2020). In the Philippine context, students frequently engage with social media, online messaging applications, and digital learning platforms, which increases their exposure to potential cyber threats (Reyes, 2019). Because of this growing digital presence, improving cybersecurity awareness among students is essential for maintaining a safe educational environment.

Another important concept related to cybersecurity is safe digital practice. Safe digital practices refer to responsible behaviors that individuals follow when using digital technologies, including avoiding suspicious links, protecting personal information, and updating software regularly. According to Abd Rahim et al. (2015), practicing safe digital habits significantly reduces the likelihood of cyber threats and promotes a safer digital environment. These habits may include enabling multi-factor authentication, using antivirus software, and avoiding downloads from unknown sources. By developing strong cybersecurity awareness and consistently applying safe digital practices, students can protect themselves from various forms of cybercrime and contribute to a more secure online community.
