What is a Data Breach?

A data breach occurs when sensitive or confidential information is accessed, stolen, or exposed without authorization. These incidents typically happen when cybercriminals exploit security weaknesses in digital systems or databases that store large amounts of personal data. According to cybersecurity experts, data breaches can expose information such as usernames, passwords, financial records, and personal identification details. When this information becomes publicly available or falls into the hands of attackers, individuals may face identity theft, financial fraud, or privacy violations. Data breaches can affect both organizations and individual users who rely on digital services.

Students may be affected by data breaches when online platforms they use experience security failures that expose user information. For example, if a social media platform or online learning system is compromised, attackers may obtain the personal data of thousands of users. This information can then be used to conduct phishing attacks, impersonation, or identity theft. Using strong passwords and enabling additional security features such as two-factor authentication can help protect accounts even when data breaches occur. Staying informed about cybersecurity risks also allows users to respond quickly if their personal information becomes exposed.
