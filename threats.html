Common Cybersecurity Threats

Cybersecurity threats refer to malicious activities that attempt to damage digital systems, steal sensitive information, or gain unauthorized access to personal accounts. These threats are becoming more common as individuals increasingly rely on internet-connected devices for communication, education, and entertainment. Students who frequently use social media platforms, online messaging applications, and digital learning tools may unknowingly expose themselves to various cybersecurity risks. According to Rahman et al. (2020), increasing cybersecurity awareness significantly reduces the likelihood of becoming a victim of fraud, identity theft, or cyberbullying. Understanding how cyber threats operate is therefore an essential step in protecting personal data and maintaining a secure online environment.

Cyber threats can appear in many different forms, ranging from deceptive online messages to malicious software that infects devices. Some attacks attempt to trick users into sharing sensitive information, while others focus on gaining unauthorized access to accounts or monitoring online activities without the user's knowledge. Research also shows that cyber threats can negatively affect students’ emotional well-being, especially when they involve harassment or cyberbullying (Delos Santos, 2021). Because of these risks, it is important for students to recognize common types of cyber threats and learn how to avoid them through safe digital practices. Education and awareness programs in schools can help students understand these risks and develop responsible online behavior.

This website provides detailed explanations of several common cybersecurity threats that students may encounter while using digital technologies. Each threat is discussed on a separate page in order to provide a clear explanation, examples, and prevention strategies. The threats covered include phishing, malware, identity theft, cyberbullying, ransomware, spyware, account hacking, social engineering, password attacks, spam attacks, fake websites, and data breaches. By understanding these threats and learning how they operate, students can improve their cybersecurity awareness and take proactive steps to protect themselves online. The following pages will explore each threat in detail and provide guidance on how individuals can prevent these attacks from affecting their digital lives.
