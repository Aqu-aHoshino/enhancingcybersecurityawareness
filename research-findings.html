Summary of Research Results

The research conducted among senior high school students at Lian Institute Inc. examined their level of cybersecurity awareness and familiarity with common digital threats. The results indicated that many students are aware of common online risks such as phishing and malware but still lack deeper knowledge regarding how these threats operate and how they can be prevented. Several respondents reported that they have encountered suspicious messages, spam emails, or fraudulent links while using social media platforms and online communication tools. These findings suggest that although students regularly interact with digital technologies, their understanding of cybersecurity practices remains limited. Increasing cybersecurity awareness programs within schools could therefore significantly improve students' ability to identify and avoid cyber threats.

Another important finding of the study is that many students rely primarily on basic security measures such as simple passwords or default privacy settings. While these measures provide some level of protection, they may not be sufficient against more advanced cyber attacks. The study also found that only a small number of students actively use additional security tools such as antivirus software or two-factor authentication. This indicates a need for improved cybersecurity education that teaches students not only about digital threats but also about practical protective strategies. By strengthening cybersecurity education programs, schools can help students develop safer digital habits and improve their ability to respond to online security risks.
