What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a form of malicious software that prevents users from accessing their files or computer systems until a payment is made to the attacker. Once ransomware infects a device, it typically encrypts important files, making them inaccessible to the victim. Cybercriminals then demand a ransom payment in exchange for providing the decryption key required to restore the files. According to cybersecurity studies, ransomware attacks have increased significantly in recent years and often target individuals, businesses, and educational institutions. Victims who fail to pay the ransom may permanently lose access to their data.

Students may encounter ransomware when downloading infected files, opening malicious email attachments, or visiting compromised websites. For example, a user might receive an email claiming to contain an important document, but opening the attachment installs ransomware on the device. Once the system is infected, a warning message may appear demanding payment to unlock the files. Regularly backing up important data and avoiding suspicious downloads are effective strategies for preventing ransomware damage. Maintaining updated security software can also help detect and block ransomware before it spreads.
