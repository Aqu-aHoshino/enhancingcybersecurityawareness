What are Password Attacks?

Password attacks are cybersecurity threats where attackers attempt to gain unauthorized access to digital accounts by discovering or guessing user passwords. Cybercriminals often use automated tools that attempt thousands of password combinations within seconds, making weak or common passwords easy targets. According to cybersecurity research, password attacks are among the most common methods used to compromise user accounts because many individuals reuse passwords across multiple platforms. When attackers successfully guess a password, they may gain access to personal files, private messages, and financial information stored in the account. This type of attack can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and unauthorized use of personal accounts.

Students frequently become victims of password attacks when they create simple passwords that are easy to remember but also easy for attackers to guess.
