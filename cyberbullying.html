What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying refers to the use of digital platforms such as social media, messaging applications, and online forums to harass, threaten, or humiliate another person. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can occur at any time and may spread quickly through online communities. According to Delos Santos (2021), cyberbullying can have serious emotional and psychological effects on students, including stress, anxiety, and reduced academic performance. Because digital communication allows individuals to remain anonymous, some attackers may feel encouraged to engage in harmful behavior without immediate consequences. As a result, victims may feel isolated or afraid to participate in online environments.

Students may experience cyberbullying through negative comments, offensive messages, or the public sharing of embarrassing photos and information. For example, an individual might repeatedly send harmful messages or post insulting comments on a student's social media profile. In some cases, attackers may create fake accounts to spread rumors or damage the victim’s reputation. Preventing cyberbullying requires promoting respectful online behavior and encouraging students to report harmful interactions when they occur. Schools and families also play an important role in supporting victims and educating students about responsible digital communication.
