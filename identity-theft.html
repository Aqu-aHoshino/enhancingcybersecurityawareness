What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft occurs when cybercriminals obtain and use another person's personal information without permission, usually for fraudulent activities. This information may include names, addresses, passwords, social security numbers, or financial details that can be used to impersonate the victim online. According to Rahman et al. (2020), identity theft has become increasingly common due to the widespread sharing of personal data across social media platforms and digital services. Once attackers obtain this information, they may create fake accounts, access financial services, or conduct illegal transactions under the victim’s identity. Victims of identity theft may experience financial loss, privacy violations, and reputational damage.

Students sometimes become victims of identity theft when they overshare personal information on social media platforms. For example, publicly posting details such as full names, birthdays, school locations, or personal photos can give cybercriminals enough information to impersonate the user online. Attackers may use this information to create fake accounts or trick others into believing they are communicating with the real person. Preventing identity theft requires careful management of personal information and the use of privacy settings on social networking platforms. Limiting the amount of personal data shared online can significantly reduce the risk of identity theft.
