What are Fake Websites?

Fake websites are fraudulent online pages created to imitate legitimate websites in order to deceive users into providing sensitive information. These websites often copy the appearance, logos, and design of trusted platforms such as banks, social media sites, or online stores. Cybercriminals use fake websites to collect login credentials, financial information, or personal data entered by unsuspecting users. According to cybersecurity studies, fake websites are frequently used in phishing attacks where victims are directed to the fraudulent page through email or message links. Once the victim enters their information, the data is immediately captured by the attacker.

Students may encounter fake websites when searching for free downloads, educational resources, or online services. For example, a fake login page that looks identical to a real social media platform may trick users into entering their account credentials. After submitting the information, attackers can use the stolen credentials to access the real account. Carefully checking website URLs and avoiding suspicious links can help prevent this type of cyberattack. Secure websites typically use HTTPS encryption, which can be verified through the browser address bar.
